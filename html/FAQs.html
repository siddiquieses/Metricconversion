A concise list of common questions and answers designed to provide quick solutions, clarify information, and address common concerns about a product, service, or topic.

Q.1 How do I enter numbers in Scientific Notation?

Scientific notation is a way to express very large or very small numbers in a simplified form. In scientific notation, a number is written as the product of a coefficient and a power of 10. For example:

1,200,000 → 1.2 × 10⁶

0.000045 → 4.5 × 10⁻⁵

Use “E” notation (e.g., 1.2E6) on most calculators/websites.

Q.2 What is a knot? What is a nautical mile?

A knot is a unit of speed used in maritime and aviation, equal to one nautical mile per hour. A nautical mile is approximately 1.852 km or 1.15078 miles.

Q.3 How do I convert between inches of rain and millimeters?

To convert inches to millimeters:

1 inch = 25.4 mm → 2 inches × 25.4 = 50.8 mm.

Q.4 What is the difference between long ton, short ton, and metric ton?

Long ton (UK): 2,240 pounds (1,016 kg)

Short ton (US): 2,000 pounds (907.18474 kg)

Metric ton: 1,000 kilograms (2,204.62 pounds)

Q.5 At what temperature are Celsius and Fahrenheit the same?

Celsius and Fahrenheit align at -40°, meaning:

-40°C = -40°F
